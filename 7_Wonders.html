Seven Wonders of the World

Great Pyramid of Giza

The Great Pyramid of Giza is the oldest and largest of the three pyramids in the Giza pyramid complex in Egypt. It is the only remaining wonder of the ancient world and is believed to have been built as a tomb for Pharaoh Khufu in the 26th century BCE.

Great Wall of China

The Great Wall of China is a series of fortifications built across the historical northern borders of China to protect against various invading forces. It is the longest wall in the world and is recognized as one of the greatest architectural feats in history.

Petra

Petra is an ancient city in Jordan that was carved into the rock face by the Nabataean people. It is famous for its unique rock-cut architecture and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Colosseum

The Colosseum is an amphitheatre in Rome, Italy, that was used for gladiatorial contests and public spectacles. It is one of the most iconic symbols of the Roman Empire and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is a white marble mausoleum in Agra, India, that was built by Mughal emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. It is regarded as one of the finest examples of Mughal architecture and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

Christ the Redeemer

Christ the Redeemer is a statue of Jesus Christ in Rio de Janeiro, Brazil, that stands at 30 meters tall. It was completed in 1931 and has since become a symbol of Christianity and a popular tourist attraction.
